PAN ASEAN Coalition for Epidemic and OUtbreak Preparedness (PACE-UP)

PACE-UP AT A GLANCE

The PAN ASEAN Coalition for Epidemic and OUtbreak Preparedness (PACE-UP) is a multidisciplinary consortium of 28 institutions from North-South and South-South collaborations involving local, regional, and international stakeholders. PACE-UP bundles its activities at the Vietnamese-German Center for Medical Research (VG-CARE) in Hanoi, Vietnam. VG-CARE was selected by the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD) with funds provided by the German Foreign Office (AA) as one of four Global Health Centres for pandemic and outbreak preparedness and response to infectious diseases. Rapid and uncontrolled spread of infectious diseases in global dimensions as currently experienced in the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic massively affects human health and other social and economic aspects. Our consortium utilizes our ethos and tracks record of equitable Asian-European-African partnerships to build effective outbreak response capacities, aligned closely to research and training across all geographical regions in ASEAN countries. The project is led by Prof. Dr. Thirumalaisamy P. Velavan and Prof. Dr. Le Huu Song, Directors of VG-CARE.

WHO WE ARE

28 institutions from 15 partner countries work together within the PACE-UP coalition. The well-established and successful North-South and South-South collaborative networks bring into the PACE-UP a comprehensive, multidisciplinary and extensive set of expertise skills and experience covering public health expertise, capacity development, clinical trials, ethics of research in emergency situations, surveillance of emerging and re-emerging zoonosis, molecular biology, epidemiology, rapid diagnostics, rapid whole genome sequencing, phylogenetic analyses and pathogen evolutionary trends, infection control, microbiology, virology, immunology, data analyses, rapid analysis publication and dissemination of data, risk communication, management, operational logistics, patient and community engagement, advocacy, global policy issues, disease modelling, trends and prediction. The PIs and consortia partners have highlighted the need to obtain unity of purpose on emerging infections.

OBJECTIVES

CAPACITY BUILDING


Our overall aim is to strengthen regional and PAN-ASEAN individual, institutional and infrastructural / organizational capacities and systems for enabling rapid and effective response during an emergency situation / epidemic situation and strengthen the capabilities to identify new capacities during the inter-epidemic period. We are taking forward our PACE-UP consortium vision to provide high quality mentorship by various educational and training programs. With North-South and South-South partnerships, we envisage to strengthen the academic sector by increasing quality access to higher education through scholarships for study in Germany and in the African regions.

MORE

NETWORK / DATA EXCHANGE


PACE-UP network with other regional and global networks on emerging infections and contribute to global preparedness and response activities as well as rapid implementation of findings into optimized clinical and public health practices by maximizing synergies. Effective communication channels between participating partners are being implemented to ensure secure and fast data exchange between different partners and countries.

SUPPORTING STAKEHOLDERS


Our team of recognized experts has experience in engaging the stakeholders and disseminating research findings. We fully involve politicians, policy makers and stakeholders as well as global public health agencies during all stages of our program and activities and make our expertise available to advise and support the decision-makers in the development and implementation of prevention and response strategies.

ACTIVITIES
DOCTORAL STUDIES
  • 6 fulltime PhD (4 Vietnamese and 2 African) students will conduct their studies in Experimental Medicine program at the Medical Faculty of the University of Tübingen.
  • The study starts on 01.01.2022 and lusts for max. 4 years.
  • PhD students will undertake research rotations between African-Asian and German institutions.
MASTER’S PROJECT MODULES
  • 4 Master Degree project modules a year will take place at VG-CARE.
  • Participating Master students come from different regional partners.
  • Aim: Receiving of the Master’s degree along with the project module done in VG-CARE from the respective university of the candidate.
INTERNSHIPS
(ASEAN-GERMANY-AFRICA)
  • Internships in Germany will rotate between German partner sites on distinct needs of infectious disease research capacity.
  • Distinct and tailored internships are aimed for 12 MDs/MSc/PhDs from Asia and 4 MDs/MSc/PhDs from Africa.
  • The Asian candidates will spend 3 months in Germany and 2 months at an African partner site, whereas the African candidates will spend 3 months in Germany and 2 months at an Asian partner site.
TRAINING WORKSHOPS ON
SKILLS AND COMPETENCIES
  • One training workshop every year.
  • Target group: front-line personnel from national referral centers and reference laboratories, targeting physicians, nurses and laboratory scientists, who will have to provide diagnostic services and clinical care to emerging infectious disease patients.
  • Topics: practical training on how to safely collect, package, label, ship, store and process clinical specimens, practical training on how to don and doff personal protective equipment (PPE), the diagnostic technologies for outbreak settings, and how to provide clinical care.
TRAINING WORKSHOPS ON ADVANCED
RESEARCH KNOWLEDGE AND SKILLS
  • One training workshop every year.
  • Target group: research active personnel with post-graduate qualifications (MD, MSc, MPH, PhD), including physicians, veterinarians, entomologists, biomedical scientists and epidemiologists.
  • Topics: surveillance models and methods, ethical considerations, regulatory concepts, clinical trial design and implementation in outbreak settings, epidemiology, innovative approaches to data collection and analysis and possible synergies with well-funded and well-established on-going infectious disease control programs and more advanced clinical care.
DATA MANAGEMENT
  • Creation of a secured web-based data management platform shared with all partners.
  • Developing of the common data management procedures and protocols across the PACE-UP coalition:
    • standardized clinical and surveillance data
    • newly available web-based tools and data analysis
    • development of mathematical models to monitor in real-time the impact of an emerging infection outbreak and to predict its potential evolution
  • Supporting rapid data sharing during disease outbreaks in collaboration with national and supranational partners such as regional WHO and local CDCs.
EXPERT SEMINARS ON GCP/GCLP
  • 2 days seminar each alternative year on distinct topics.
  • With support of lecturers from the Institute for Tropical Medicine, University of Tübingen, Germany.
  • Basic and advanced modules.
EXPERT SEMINARS ON ETHICS
AND REGULATORY CONCEPRS
  • Aim: Developing capacities for ethical and regulatory aspects of research in emergency situations to cover ethics of research in emergency situations and developing ethics expertise.
  • Each team will have a nominated ethics expert, with personalized continuing professional development.
SHORT TERM EXPERT/MENTOR
  • An infectious diseases specialist shall strengthen the individual and institutional capacities with his experience and wisdom.
  • 4 stays per year for 15 days in Vietnam.
THREE PILLARS FOR CAPACITY BUILDING
EDUCATION AND TRAINING

Strengthen the academic sector by providing access to higher education study programs through scholarships for study in Germany and in the regions and by intensifying local scientific expertise through a North-South and South-South partnership.

ACTIVITIES:

  • Trans-interdisciplinary stipends
  • Mentorships
  • MD/PhDs
  • Increasing the laboratory capacities
  • Increasing of the critical mass of trainers in the region
RESEARCH CAPACITIES

Develop capacities for conduct of research (multidisciplinary operational, social science, basic science, translational clinical, clinical trials, implementation research).

RESEARCH AREAS:

  • Epidemiological surveillance
  • Diagnostics spectrum
  • Clinical research
  • Artificial intelligence
  • Digital epidemiology
HEALTH CARE

Develop and setup robust ‘ready to go within 48-72 hours’ PACE-UP outbreak rapid response teams to assist when an outbreak occurs.

  • Health care workers
  • Infection control
  • Patient engagement
  • Risk communication
  • Community
